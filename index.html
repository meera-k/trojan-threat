Trojan Threats

What are Trojan attacks?

Trojan horses, or Trojan attacks, are a type of malware that often tricks users by disguising itself as legitimate software. Typically, users are tricked into installing it through some form of social engineering. After a Trojan has been installed and executed on a user's system, cyber criminals can gain backdoor access to your system, allowing them to delete, block, modify, or copy data.

Trojans are unable to self-replicate or execute themselves, unlike many other types of computer viruses, yet they can still cause many types of damages to your system.

Some types of Trojans include Backdoor Trojans, which allow a criminal to gain remote access over your computer, DDoS Trojans, which perform DDoS attacks and take down networks by flooding them with traffic, Banker Trojans, which aim to steal your account data for online banking systems, and many more.

You can protect yourself from Trojans by keeping your operating system up-to-date, using firewalls to protect your personal information, and by using effective anti-malware software.
